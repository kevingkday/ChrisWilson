Mortgage FAQ

What is the mortgage stress test?

add

In Canada, you must qualify at either the contract rate + 2% or the benchmark rate (whichever is higher). I help you understand the impact on your borrowing power and explore strategies — like longer amortizations or alternative lenders — to help you qualify for the home you want.

How much down payment do I need?

add

The minimum down payment in Canada is 5% for homes under $500,000, 10% on the portion between $500K–$1M, and 20% for homes over $1M. However, putting down more or less can significantly impact your rate and insurance costs — I'll help you find the optimal balance.

Should I choose variable or fixed rate?

add

It depends on your risk tolerance and financial goals. Fixed rates offer payment certainty; variable rates can save you money over the long term but carry some risk. I'll analyze your situation and walk you through the pros and cons of each option.

Does using a mortgage broker cost me anything?

add

In most cases, my services are completely free to you. I'm compensated by the lender when your mortgage is funded. In some special circumstances (private lending, complex files), there may be a broker fee — but I'll always be transparent about costs upfront.
